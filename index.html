Motorcycle Insurance Quote

Rider Information
💡 MSF = Motorcycle Safety Foundation. A safety course that can qualify for a discount.
Motorcycle Details
Coverage Requested
📖 Coverage Explanations — If client asks:
Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist: Protects you if you're hit by a driver with no insurance or not enough insurance to cover your damages.
Medical Payments (MedPay): Pays your medical bills after an accident regardless of who was at fault — even a fall from the bike.
PIP (Personal Injury Protection): Similar to MedPay but broader — covers lost wages and rehab too, not just medical bills.
Roadside Assistance: If the bike breaks down, we arrange a tow or help. Usually very affordable to add.
Modifications?
💡 Modifications can increase the bike's value and may affect the premium. Ask the client if the bike has any aftermarket changes.

📖 Coverage Explanations — If client asks:
Personal Property Coverage: Covers your belongings (furniture, electronics, clothes) if they're stolen or damaged by fire, water, etc.

High-Value Items Rider: Standard policy has limits on jewelry/electronics. A rider adds extra coverage for specific expensive items.

Business Insurance Quote

Business Information
💡 What exactly does this business DO day-to-day? The more specific, the better for our agents to match coverage.
💡 Subcontractors = people they hire to do parts of jobs (not employees).
Business History
💡 Did any carrier ever cancel their policy or refuse to renew it? This is important info for underwriting.
💡 Gov contracts often require higher liability limits and specific endorsements.
Coverage Needed
📖 Coverage Explanations — If client asks:
General Liability (GL): Covers bodily injury or property damage caused to others by your business. Example: client trips and falls at your job site.
Commercial Property: Covers the building and/or contents (equipment, inventory) from fire, theft, storms, etc.
BOP (Business Owner's Policy): A bundle of GL + Commercial Property — usually more affordable than buying separately. Best for small businesses.
Workers Comp: Covers your employees if they get injured on the job — pays medical bills and lost wages. In Texas it's optional but highly recommended.
Professional Liability / E&O: If a client claims your advice or service caused them financial harm, this covers the lawsuit. Common for consultants, IT, accountants.
Cyber Liability: Covers data breaches, hacking, ransomware. Important if the business handles customer data or takes online payments.
⚠️ Workers Comp — Texas is Special
Texas is the only state where WC is NOT mandatory. However many contracts and job sites REQUIRE it. Collect this extra info:
💡 Subcontractors without their own WC policy may be treated as employees by the insurer.
🏗️ Commercial Property — Additional Details
💡 Having sprinklers usually lowers the premium significantly.
Current Insurance

Commercial Auto Insurance Quote

Business Info
💡 DOT number is required for vehicles over 10,001 lbs used in interstate commerce.
Drivers
💡 All drivers who may operate a covered vehicle must be listed — even occasional drivers.
Vehicles
Operations
💡 This affects premium significantly — longer hauls = higher risk.
💡 Oil & gas sites often require specific endorsements and higher limits.
💡 SR-22 = proof of financial responsibility. MCS-90 = required for FMCSA motor carriers.
Coverage
📖 Coverage Explanations — If client asks:
Liability: Covers damage or injury you cause to others with the vehicle. Required by law. Limit = max the policy will pay.
Physical Damage (Comp/Collision): Covers your own vehicle. Comp = theft/weather/animals. Collision = crash damage.
Cargo Coverage: Covers the goods or equipment being transported if damaged or stolen in transit.
MCS-90 Filing: Federal endorsement required for certain carriers proving they have minimum liability coverage — the government can collect directly from the insurer.
📦 Cargo Coverage Details
💡 Hazmat = chemicals, flammables, explosives. Requires special license and endorsements.
Prior Insurance
💡 This is a major underwriting flag. Ask clearly but without pressure.
